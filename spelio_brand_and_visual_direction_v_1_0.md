# Spelio — Brand & Visual Direction v1.0

## Purpose

This document defines the intended visual identity, emotional tone, interaction feel, presentation philosophy, and overall brand direction for Spelio.

It exists to:

- prevent visual and brand drift
- guide future frontend and UX decisions
- guide AI-assisted design generation
- support future marketing work
- support future agency/freelancer collaboration
- clarify how Spelio should feel visually and emotionally
- separate interaction calmness from stereotypical visual interpretations

This document should be read alongside:

- Spelio MVP Product Specification
- Spelio Product Philosophy & Direction Notes
- Spelio Content System Specification
- Spelio Launch & Marketing Roadmap
- Schools & Educational Expansion Plan

This document is intentionally higher-level than the MVP specification.

The MVP specification defines:

- behaviour
- UX logic
- interaction rules
- technical constraints
- progression systems

This document defines:

- visual interpretation
- emotional tone
- presentation philosophy
- interaction feel
- aesthetic direction
- brand personality
- visual references
- what Spelio is and is not visually

---

# 1. Core Brand Direction

Spelio should feel like:

> a modern, focused, premium language-learning utility built around spelling confidence and practical recall.

The product should communicate:

- clarity
- confidence
- focus
- precision
- intelligence
- quality
- calm competence
- modernity
- usability
- trustworthiness

The visual tone should feel:

- contemporary
- refined
- mature
- lightweight
- responsive
- tactile
- carefully designed
- cognitively clean

Spelio should not feel:

- childish
- noisy
- chaotic
- over-gamified
- rustic
- overly soft
- whimsical
- countryside-themed
- folk-themed
- meditation-inspired
- wellness-branded
- tourism-oriented
- heavily academic

The goal is:

> focused spelling practice with premium interaction quality.

---

# 2. Calmness Clarification

Calmness is one of Spelio’s most important principles.

However, calmness refers primarily to:

- interaction design
- cognitive load
- focus management
- emotional safety
- clarity of use
- low-friction repetition

Calmness does NOT mean:

- countryside branding
- mountains and valleys
- nature-heavy visuals
- eco-brand aesthetics
- meditation-app styling
- wellness-product branding
- slow or sleepy interfaces
- decorative serenity
- rustic Welsh identity systems
- handcrafted folk aesthetics

Spelio should not visually drift toward:

- tourism branding
- mindfulness branding
- yoga/wellness product aesthetics
- heritage-craft branding
- fantasy-learning visuals
- forest/mountain imagery as dominant identity

Calmness means:

- low distraction
- restrained animation
- clear hierarchy
- subtle transitions
- minimal interruptions
- emotionally safe feedback
- visually controlled interfaces
- focused user attention
- efficient understanding
- low cognitive overload

The intended emotional feeling is:

> quiet confidence rather than softness.

---

# 3. Product Personality

If Spelio were described as a product personality, it would feel:

- thoughtful
- capable
- intelligent
- calm under pressure
- minimal but not empty
- elegant but practical
- modern but not trend-chasing
- premium without arrogance
- focused rather than simplistic

Spelio should feel more like:

- a precision tool
- a modern productivity product
- a high-quality learning utility

than:

- an educational toy
- a classroom worksheet system
- a gamified entertainment product

# 4. Visual Reference Direction

Spelio’s design language should sit closer to clean modern software products than traditional educational software.

Useful reference directions include:

- Linear
- Notion
- Arc Browser
- Raycast
- Readwise
- Apple educational product pages
- high-end productivity tools

Important reference qualities:

- clear and well-balanced typography
- elegant spacing
- restrained colour usage
- clean interaction hierarchy
- subtle motion
- premium tactility
- refined surfaces
- modern minimalism
- clarity before decoration

Spelio should NOT visually resemble:

- Duolingo
- traditional phonics apps
- children’s educational games
- countryside tourism websites
- eco retreat branding
- meditation apps
- fantasy-language-learning products
- cartoon-heavy edtech

---

# 5. Typography Direction

Typography should support clarity, usability, and calm focus throughout the product.

The product should feel:

- readable
- modern
- restrained
- premium

Typography should prioritise:

- clarity
- rhythm
- spacing
- hierarchy
- readability
- elegance

Avoid:

- bubbly education fonts
- novelty fonts
- overly playful typography
- cartoon-like rounded headings
- exaggerated learning-app typography

The typography system should feel closer to:

- modern productivity software

rather than:

- worksheets
- primary-school posters
- phonics games

Spelio typography should feel clear, modern, restrained, and highly readable. The goal is not expressive typography or design-showcase styling. Typography should quietly support usability, hierarchy, and focus rather than becoming a dominant visual feature.

---

# 6. Colour Philosophy

Spelio uses restrained colour rather than highly expressive colour.

The product should primarily rely on:

- warm neutrals
- soft contrast
- restrained accent colours
- clean surfaces
- subtle layering
- carefully controlled emphasis

The visual system should feel:

- premium
- mature
- lightweight
- refined

not:

- vibrant for the sake of excitement
- heavily saturated
- overly playful
- colour-noisy

Important clarification:

Warm neutrals do NOT imply:

- eco palettes
- moss greens
- countryside colour systems
- earthy handmade branding
- rustic aesthetics

Colour should support:

- readability
- focus
- confidence
- emotional comfort
- long-term usability

rather than:

- thematic decoration
- emotional manipulation
- artificial excitement

# 7. Interaction Philosophy

Spelio’s interaction feel is one of its most important differentiators.

The product should feel:

- tactile
- responsive
- smooth
- satisfying
- immediate
- controlled
- lightweight
- predictable

Feedback should feel:

- subtle
- precise
- intentional
- restrained
- useful

Interaction design should avoid:

- dopamine-heavy effects
- exaggerated celebration
- noisy transitions
- excessive animation
- reward explosions
- attention-hacking mechanics
- visual chaos

The ideal interaction feeling is:

> satisfying competence.

The product should feel rewarding because it is:

- clear
- usable
- responsive
- psychologically comfortable

—not because it overwhelms users with stimulation.

---

# 8. Motion & Animation

Animation should support clarity and tactility.

Motion should feel:

- restrained
- polished
- modern
- subtle
- responsive

Good animation uses:

- gentle easing
- micro-feedback
- state clarity
- soft transitions
- motion that reinforces interaction understanding

Avoid:

- bounce-heavy interfaces
- cartoon motion
- exaggerated celebration
- long animations
- flashy transitions
- attention-grabbing effects

Animation should rarely be decorative.

It should primarily support:

- usability
- feedback
- focus
- interaction understanding

# 9. Educational Positioning

Spelio should feel educationally useful without visually becoming:

- school software
- worksheet software
- classroom management software
- a children’s learning game

The strongest educational positioning is:

> serious but lightweight.

This means:

- approachable
- practical
- low-friction
- focused
- cognitively clean

not:

- cartoon-driven
- reward-heavy
- childish
- over-explained

The product should feel equally appropriate for:

- adults
- GCSE learners
- older independent learners
- teachers
- dyslexic learners

without visually fragmenting into separate identities.

---

# 10. Welsh Identity Direction

Spelio is proudly Welsh-focused.

Welsh identity should emerge primarily through language, usefulness, tone, and thoughtful design rather than relying heavily on visual clichés or tourism-style imagery.

The product should avoid over-reliance on:

- dragons
- mountains
- valleys
- slate textures
- folk motifs
- Celtic ornamentation
- tourism imagery
- heritage-branding tropes

Spelio should feel confidently Welsh through authentic language-learning usefulness rather than decorative symbolism.

---

# 11. Accessibility Direction

Accessibility should integrate naturally into the premium design system.

Accessibility does NOT mean:

- visually loud interfaces
- oversized childish UI
- ugly enterprise accessibility modes
- abandoning refined design

Spelio should aim for:

- elegant accessibility
- resilient layouts
- strong contrast where needed
- readable typography
- keyboard accessibility
- scalable interfaces
- low cognitive load
- emotionally safe interaction

Accessibility should strengthen the product’s calmness and clarity rather than conflict with them.

---

# 12. Marketing & Landing Page Direction

Marketing materials should communicate:

- usefulness
- clarity
- confidence
- premium quality
- focused practice
- intelligent learning design

Avoid:

- startup hype language
- aggressive productivity messaging
- “hack your learning” language
- countryside lifestyle branding
- overly soft wellness copy

The visual direction for marketing should feel:

- modern
- editorial
- product-focused
- typographically strong
- visually restrained
- interaction-led

The app itself should remain the hero.

Marketing visuals should prioritise:

- real interface mockups
- interaction moments
- progress feedback
- focused practice
- clear typography
- practical benefits

rather than abstract scenery or emotional filler imagery.

---

# 13. Long-Term Brand Direction

As Spelio grows, the visual system should remain coherent.

Future expansions may include:

- GCSE products
- school tools
- additional languages
- teacher systems
- accessibility enhancements
- AI-assisted reinforcement
- speaking practice
- classroom features

However, the core brand identity should remain:

- focused
- intelligent
- premium
- calm
- tactile
- modern
- useful
- cognitively clean

The product should evolve through:

- refinement
- depth
- capability
- polish

—not through increasing visual noise.

---

# 14. Final Brand Principle

Spelio should feel like:

> a beautifully crafted modern learning utility that respects the learner’s intelligence, attention, and time.

The strongest long-term advantage is not visual spectacle.

It is:

- clarity
- usefulness
- trust
- interaction quality
- focused repetition
- emotionally safe learning
- calm confidence
- refined design
