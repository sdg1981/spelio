# 1. Content Philosophy

## 1.1 Purpose

The Welsh Spelling Basics system is a lightweight educational support layer around the core Spelio spelling-practice engine.

Its purpose is to:
- reduce intimidation around Welsh spelling
- build spelling confidence
- explain common Welsh spelling patterns simply
- support adult learners who want a little more understanding
- reinforce the core hear → recall → spell loop

The system exists to support spelling practice, not replace it.

---

## 1.2 Core educational principle

The content should feel like:

> Helpful observations about Welsh spelling.

NOT:
- lessons
- modules
- curriculum units
- grammar chapters
- formal tutorials

---

## 1.3 Product positioning

The educational layer must always remain secondary to spelling practice.

The primary Spelio experience remains:

> hear → recall → spell → feedback → repeat

The spelling basics layer:
- supports
- reinforces
- explains
- contextualises

It must never dominate the product identity.

---

# 2. Editorial Tone Rules

## 2.1 Tone goals

Content should feel:
- calm
- adult-oriented
- lightweight
- modern
- practical
- reassuring
- concise

---

## 2.2 Avoid

Avoid:
- linguistic jargon
- IPA notation
- academic explanations
- grammar-heavy teaching
- classroom tone
- over-explaining
- dense paragraphs
- patronising beginner language
- “fun mascot” educational energy

---

## 2.3 Preferred writing style

Prefer:
- short paragraphs
- direct observations
- examples first
- practical recognition cues
- pattern-based teaching
- gentle confidence-building

Good:
> “DD makes the soft ‘th’ sound in this.”

Bad:
> “DD represents a voiced dental fricative.”

---

## 2.4 Emotional tone

The learner should feel:
- capable
- reassured
- supported
- curious

Never:
- tested
- overwhelmed
- lectured
- corrected harshly

# 5. Topic Content Specifications

# 5.1 DD

## Purpose

Explain the Welsh `dd` sound simply.

---

## Core learner takeaway

`dd` sounds like the “th” in:
- this
- that
- there

---

## Subtitle

### English
A soft, gentle sound

### Welsh
Sain feddal, dyner

---

## Main explanation

### English
The letters dd make a soft voiced “th” sound, like the th in this.

It’s one of the most common sounds in Welsh and appears in many everyday words.

---

### Welsh
Mae'r llythrennau dd yn gwneud sain “th” feddal, fel y th yn y gair Saesneg this.

Mae'n un o'r seiniau mwyaf cyffredin yn Gymraeg ac mae'n ymddangos mewn llawer o eiriau bob dydd.

---

## Example words

- dydd
- heddiw
- brawd

---

## Tip

### English
When you see dd in a word, think of the “th” sound in this.

### Welsh
Pan welwch dd mewn gair, meddyliwch am y sain “th” yn y gair Saesneg this.

---


# 6. Bilingual Content Rules

## 6.1 Both interface languages are first-class

All learner-facing spelling basics content should support:
- English
- Cymraeg

---

## 6.2 Welsh tone

Welsh content should remain:
- modern
- lightweight
- non-academic
- readable
- concise

Avoid:
- overly formal Welsh
- policy-document Welsh
- textbook tone

---

## 6.3 Welsh examples

Do not translate the actual Welsh spelling examples themselves.

Example:
- dydd
- heddiw
- brawd

remain unchanged in both language modes.

---

## 6.4 English gloss handling in Welsh mode

The Welsh interface may:
- hide English glosses
OR
- reduce their prominence

depending on layout needs.

This may evolve later.

---

# 9. Future Expansion Boundaries

## 9.1 Future topics may include

- mutations
- apostrophes
- North/South Welsh
- common spelling traps
- vowel combinations
- sentence moments

Future versions of Spelio may explore context-dependent spelling patterns, for example Welsh mutations, where correct written Welsh depends on neighbouring words. This would be approached from a spelling-confidence perspective rather than as a comprehensive grammar curriculum. Any future mutation content should stay scoped around spelling mechanics, recognition, and written accuracy.

---

## 9.2 Avoid feature sprawl

Do NOT turn the system into:
- a Welsh course
- a grammar encyclopedia
- an educational dashboard
- a tutorial tree

---

## 9.3 Quality over quantity

The system should prioritise:
- clarity
- confidence
- restraint
- usefulness

5 excellent topics are better than:
- 40 rushed explanations
- repetitive filler
- textbook density
