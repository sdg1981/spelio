# Spelio Practice Catalogue Draft v1

Here is a complete, structured design for the Spelio Practice Catalogue. This structure focuses heavily on clear visual hierarchy, teacher usability, and a spelling-first progression rather than vocabulary bloat.

## Catalogue Architecture

To keep the catalogue scannable for teachers and adults, the collections are divided into four high-level macro categories. Each topic within these categories follows your exact progression philosophy:

- Level 1: Predictable phonetic structures, short words, basic Welsh digraphs (e.g., LL, DD).
- Level 2: Multi-syllable words, vowel mutations, unpredictable accents (e.g., to bach / circumflex), or silent letters.
- Most Common: Pure frequency-based lists, intentionally bypassing spelling difficulty to focus on everyday utility.

## 1. Everyday Life & Identity

Focuses on immediate, concrete vocabulary that adult learners encounter daily. This forms the bedrock of early spelling confidence.

### Topic: People & Home

- People & Home 1 (Everyday Basics):
  - Purpose: Straightforward, mostly single-syllable terms for family members and rooms (e.g., mum, dad, brother, kitchen). Focuses on mastering basic initial consonants.
- People & Home 2 (Extended Environment):
  - Purpose: Introduces longer, multi-syllable household objects and extended relations where vowel combinations (like wy, ae, au) challenge spelling accuracy.
- Most Common People & Home:
  - Purpose: The absolute highest-frequency words needed to describe who you live with and where you live, regardless of spelling traps.

### Topic: Food & Drink

- Food & Drink 1 (The Essentials):
  - Purpose: Simple, highly phonetic loanwords or short native words (e.g., tea, bread, milk, apple). Great for reinforcing the single F (v sound) vs FF (f sound).
- Food & Drink 2 (Meals & Ingredients):
  - Purpose: More complex compounding and traditional Welsh terms (e.g., breakfast, vegetables) featuring trickier letter combinations like CH and RH.
- Most Common Food & Drink:
  - Purpose: The baseline vocabulary required to order in a café or read a basic Welsh menu.

### Topic: Clothing & Appearance

- Clothing 1 (Daily Wear):
  - Purpose: Accessible words for everyday clothes (e.g., hat, coat, shoes, trousers) to practice standard vowel lengths and ending consonants.
- Clothing 2 (Accessories & Textures):
  - Purpose: Words with more complex pluralization patterns or less common spelling sequences (e.g., jacket, glasses, umbrella).
- Most Common Clothing:
  - Purpose: The core clothing items most frequently used in descriptive speech.

## 2. The World Around Us

Transitions the learner from the indoor environment to external, physical surroundings. This category heavily tests the unique Welsh vowels W and Y.

### Topic: Animals

- Animals 1 (Clear Phonetics):
  - Purpose: Familiar, short animal names (e.g., dog, cat, horse, cow) that clearly demonstrate foundational letter-to-sound mapping without irregular plurals.
- Animals 2 (Spelling Patterns):
  - Purpose: Animals with challenging spelling anchors, such as the LL sound, internal Y systems, or soft mutations that often trip up spelling (e.g., pig, butterfly).
- Most Common Animals:
  - Purpose: High-frequency domestic animals and pets.
- Future Expansion Slots: Farm Animals, British Wildlife, Birds.

### Topic: Weather & Seasons

- Weather 1 (The Elements):
  - Purpose: Short, sharp weather terms (e.g., rain, sun, cold, wind) to practice basic vowel sounds and clean endings.
- Weather 2 (Atmospheric Descriptions):
  - Purpose: Longer nouns and descriptive weather states (e.g., thunderstorm, freezing, autumn) that introduce complex vowel clusters and accents.
- Most Common Weather:
  - Purpose: The essential words required to understand or give a daily Welsh weather report.

### Topic: Nature & Landscape

- Nature 1 (The Immediate Landscape):
  - Purpose: Simple geographic and natural features (e.g., sea, hill, tree, river). Focuses on short words with distinct Welsh vowels.
- Nature 2 (Flora & Terrain):
  - Purpose: More complex landforms and botanical terms (e.g., mountain, valley, forest) showcasing double consonants and long/short vowel distinctions.
- Most Common Nature:
  - Purpose: The most frequent landscape terms used in Welsh placenames and geography.

## 3. Society, Travel, & Space

Introduces wider civic vocabulary. This is highly useful for teachers aligning Spelio with official curricula (like the WJEC adult syntax frameworks).

### Topic: Places & Travel

- Places 1 (Around Town):
  - Purpose: Basic civic locations (e.g., shop, school, road, town) with reliable, phonetic spelling patterns.
- Places 2 (Transport & Destinations):
  - Purpose: Multi-syllable terms for infrastructure and transit (e.g., railway station, library, car, aeroplane) where secondary stresses challenge spelling memory.
- Most Common Places & Travel:
  - Purpose: Essential words for navigating, asking for directions, or commuting.
- Future Expansion Slots: The Welsh Landscape (Specific Placename Components like Aber-, Llan-, Pen-).

### Topic: School & Learning

- School 1 (The Pencil Case):
  - Purpose: Highly concrete classroom objects (e.g., book, pen, paper, desk) to solidify basic spelling patterns in a familiar context.
- School 2 (Subjects & Systems):
  - Purpose: Longer terms relating to education (e.g., mathematics, history, teacher, computer) featuring complex suffix endings.
- Most Common School Words:
  - Purpose: Core vocabulary required for classroom instructions and everyday learning environments.

### Topic: Work & Industry

- Work 1 (Common Roles):
  - Purpose: Straightforward, recognizable job titles and workplace spaces (e.g., office, nurse, shopkeeper, work).
- Work 2 (Professional Environments):
  - Purpose: Technically complex workplace nouns (e.g., business, technology, factory) that demand precision with Welsh suffix variations (like -aeth or -iad).
- Most Common Work Words:
  - Purpose: General employment vocabulary used in adult conversation.

## 4. Concepts & Abstracts

Rather than relying on grammatical drills (which you are intentionally avoiding for launch), this category clusters structural words by semantic themes. This provides a clean way to practice abstract spelling without heavy grammar rules.

### Topic: Time & Calendar

- Time 1 (The Days & Numbers):
  - Purpose: Days of the week and foundational numbers. Spelled strictly as vocabulary words to reinforce specific patterns like the D vs DD contrast.
- Time 2 (Months & Durations):
  - Purpose: Names of the months and terms for duration (e.g., yesterday, tomorrow, century). These contain varying syllable lengths and complex letter strings.
- Most Common Time Words:
  - Purpose: Crucial structural vocabulary for scheduling or telling the time.

### Topic: Colours & Shapes

- Colours & Shapes 1 (Primary Palettes):
  - Purpose: Basic colours (e.g., red, blue, green, white) which are excellent for learning short-vowel rules and final consonants.
- Colours & Shapes 2 (Nuance & Form):
  - Purpose: Less common shades and geometric shapes (e.g., purple, orange, circle, square) that feature irregular phonetic combinations.
- Most Common Colours:
  - Purpose: The top highest-frequency adjectives used to describe objects.

## Design Rationale

- Teacher & Adult Friendly: By avoiding abstract terms like "Core 1", a teacher can look at a student's weak spot (e.g., "They are struggling with the ch sound") and immediately look at Food & Drink 2 or Weather 2 to assign relevant topical work.
- The "Most Common" Circuit Breaker: This acts as an excellent educational escape valve. If an adult learner just wants to learn enough to survive a weekend trip or help their kid with homework, they can stick purely to the "Most Common" lists without getting bogged down by the structural progression hurdles of Level 2.
- Future Proofing: If you want to expand next year, you do not need an "Animals 3". You simply drop in a highly targeted, exciting specialist capsule list like Mythical Welsh Beasts or Coastal Wildlife directly under the "Animals" or "Nature" umbrellas.
