# Animals 2

## Status

Draft editorial exercise.

## Purpose

Introduce a second set of recognisable Welsh animal names with slightly richer spelling load than Animals 1, while remaining accessible for learners who have completed Welsh Spelling Foundations.

## Relationship to Animals 1

Animals 1 is very accessible and confidence-building, with short, highly expected animal names and no deliberate stretch words.

Animals 2 is the natural next step. It keeps the same confidence-building tone, but allows slightly longer forms, more moderate spelling-load words, and a few less immediately predictable Welsh spellings. It should still feel practical, recognisable, and tightly curated rather than like a leftover list.

## Editorial Review

### What This List Adds

Animals 2 adds several highly expected words that were not included in Animals 1, especially horse, cow, pig, duck, fox, and fish. It broadens the topic without trying to cover every animal type and keeps the list focused on vocabulary learners and teachers are likely to expect.

The list also introduces more moderate spelling-load entries than Animals 1. Hwyaden, Llwynog, Draenog, and Pysgodyn provide richer spelling practice while remaining central and accessible.

### Borderline Choices

Llwynog is the main borderline choice because the vocabulary pool marks it as Uncertain due to the alternative form Cadno. It remains in this draft because fox is a highly expected animal word and the spelling load is appropriate for Animals 2, but the Welsh form should be reviewed before approval.

Pysgodyn is also slightly borderline because it is a general animal category rather than a species. It is included because fish is highly expected, useful, and gives Animals 2 a needed sea-life anchor.

### Words Deferred

Eliffant, Gwenynen, Pili-pala, Crwban, and Mochyn daear remain good candidates but are deferred for now. They may strengthen a later list, but Animals 2 does not need to absorb every strong Core candidate.

Chicken, frog, owl, and sheep remain under review because their Welsh usage notes or uncertainty flags need more editorial confidence before placing them in a draft list beyond Animals 1.

### Spelling Progression

The progression from Animals 1 feels moderate rather than dramatic. Animals 2 keeps several Light spellings for confidence, adds a controlled group of Moderate spellings, and avoids Heavy or Very Heavy words. It should feel like the next step after Animals 1 rather than a sudden jump in difficulty.
