# Animals 3

## Status

Draft editorial exercise.

## Purpose

Introduce a third set of recognisable Welsh animal names with noticeably richer spelling load than Animals 2, while still avoiding deliberate stretch words and preserving a satisfying topic shape.

Animals 3 should broaden the Animals Topic Collection naturally. It should not become a leftover list or attempt to use every remaining Core candidate.

## Relationship to Animals 2

Animals 2 adds highly expected animal vocabulary with a moderate increase in spelling load after Animals 1.

Animals 3 may include longer Welsh forms, more vowel combinations, accents, digraphs, and less immediately predictable spellings. It should still feel accessible to learners who have completed Welsh Spelling Foundations and worked through Animals 1 and Animals 2.

## Editorial Review

### What This List Adds

Animals 3 broadens the topic after Animals 1 and Animals 2 without trying to exhaust the vocabulary pool. It adds recognisable zoo animals, reptiles, birds, sea life, and small creatures, with more moderate spelling-load entries than earlier lists.

The list keeps several light spellings so Animals 3 remains confidence-building rather than becoming a deliberate stretch list.

### Borderline Choices

Eryr and Mwnci are lighter than many possible Animals 3 candidates, but they help maintain confidence and are highly recognisable. Their inclusion also prevents Animals 3 from becoming too dense with moderate forms.

Alarch is a useful borderline entry because it is familiar and moderate rather than heavy. It gives Animals 3 richer bird vocabulary without moving into specialist bird names.

### Words Deferred

Giraffe, kangaroo, hippopotamus, porcupine, leopard, and ostrich remain plausible future candidates, but this draft avoids making Animals 3 too zoo-heavy.

Shark, whale, otter, squirrel, parrot, peacock, and crocodile also remain available in the editorial pool. They may strengthen future lists or specialist collections, but they are not required here simply because they are Core candidates.

### Spelling Progression

The progression from Animals 2 feels clear but controlled. Animals 3 includes more Moderate spellings and more varied spelling features, including hyphenation, vowel combinations, less predictable consonant patterns, and longer forms. It avoids Heavy and Very Heavy spellings, keeping the list suitable as a third introductory Animals collection rather than a specialist extension.
