## Editorial Review

### What Worked Well

The list has a clear introductory shape without becoming a pets-and-farm-only set. It includes familiar animals from domestic life, farms, British wildlife, birds, sea life, small creatures, reptiles, and zoo animals, so the topic feels broad enough for an adult learner while still staying accessible.

The spelling progression feels calm. Very Light and Light entries provide confidence, while Moderate entries introduce accents, digraphs, multi-word answers, hyphenation, and less predictable Welsh shapes in a controlled way.

### Difficult Placements

Horse, duck, sheep, chicken, fox, frog, owl, and tiger were difficult to place. They are highly recognisable or expected, but some have usage-review flags, Most Common pressure, or would shift the balance too far toward one representative role.

Bee and butterfly were included despite Moderate spelling load because they are highly recognisable and help the list avoid being dominated by mammals. Badger was included because it gives British wildlife a strong representative presence and introduces a transparent multi-word spelling pattern.

### Possible Animals 2 Moves

Mochyn daear, Gwenynen, Pili-pala, Malwen, Madfall, Brân, Alarch, Pysgodyn, and Llew may move to Animals 2 after further review if Animals 1 needs to become lighter overall. The current draft keeps them because Animals 1 should not feel like a list of only the shortest or easiest forms.

### Spelling Progression

The overall progression feels appropriate for a first Topic Collection draft. There are no Heavy or Very Heavy spellings, but the list still includes enough Moderate forms to feel like meaningful post-Foundations spelling practice rather than a simplified starter set.
