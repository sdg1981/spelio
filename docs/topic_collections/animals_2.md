# Animals 2

## Status

Approved editorial draft.

This document represents the current approved editorial version of Animals 2.

It may continue to evolve through learner and teacher feedback, but it is no longer considered a temporary draft.

## Purpose

Introduce a second set of recognisable Welsh animal vocabulary with a moderate increase in spelling load after Animals 1.

The goal is to remain confidence-building while introducing slightly richer spellings, longer forms, and highly expected animal words that were not included in Animals 1.

## Editorial Principles

* Still accessible for learners who have completed Welsh Spelling Foundations.
* Slightly richer spelling load than Animals 1.
* Highly expected animal vocabulary.
* No deliberate stretch words.
* Carefully curated rather than comprehensive.
* Approximately 10 words.

## Editorial Notes

Animals 2 intentionally introduces a moderate increase in spelling load while remaining confidence-building.

This collection reinforces several editorial principles for future Topic Collections:

* Topic expectation remains more important than perfect category balance.
* Representative Role supports editorial judgement but must not dominate selection.
* Topic 2 should feel like a natural next step, not a sudden jump in difficulty.
* A moderate spelling load is appropriate when the word is useful, recognisable, and central to the topic.
* Not every strong candidate deferred from Animals 1 must be placed in Animals 2.

The current preferred headword for fox is `Llwynog`, following the vocabulary pool. `Cadno` remains a possible future editorial alternative if later Welsh review changes the preferred form.
